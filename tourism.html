To travel within India, you’ll need a valid government-issued ID like an Aadhaar card, Voter ID, Driving License, or Passport—especially for flights, train journeys, and hotel check-ins. Keep your travel tickets and hotel booking confirmations ready, either printed or on your phone. Children may need a birth certificate or school ID. For road trips, carry your driving license, RC book, insurance, and pollution certificate. Though not always required, it’s good to keep your COVID-19 vaccination certificate and any essential medical prescriptions handy.

When traveling in India, respect local customs by dressing modestly and removing your shoes before entering temples or homes. Use your right hand for eating and giving items, as it's considered polite. Public affection is uncommon, so keep it minimal. Learn a few local greetings like “Namaste” or “Vanakkam” to connect with people. Always drink bottled water and carry hand sanitizer. Bargaining is normal in local markets, but be respectful. Book tours and services from trusted sources to avoid scams, and keep your valuables safe, especially in crowded places.

India offers a diverse travel experience across its states — Tamil Nadu is known for its magnificent temples and rich cultural routes that reflect ancient heritage. Kerala enchants visitors with its serene backwaters and rejuvenating Ayurvedic traditions. Rajasthan captivates with its majestic forts, royal palaces, and golden deserts. Himachal Pradesh, nestled in the Himalayas, is a paradise for snow lovers and thrill-seekers, offering a range of adventure sports amidst breathtaking mountain views.
